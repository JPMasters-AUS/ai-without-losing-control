# Contributing and Suggesting Changes

Thank you for wanting to improve this framework. Suggestions, corrections, and new ideas are genuinely welcome.

This is a **curated** project. One person — the maintainer — reviews every proposed change before it becomes part of the framework. That is deliberate: it keeps the documents consistent, plain-language, and coherent. Nothing changes unless the maintainer reviews and accepts it.

You do **not** need a technical background to take part. Pick whichever of the two paths below feels comfortable.

---

## How to Suggest a Change

### Path 1 — Open an "Issue" (easiest, no editing required)

Use this if you want to point something out, ask a question, or suggest an idea in your own words.

1. Go to the repository on GitHub.
2. Click the **Issues** tab near the top.
3. Click **New issue**.
4. Give it a short title and describe your suggestion in plain language. Be as specific as you can about which document and section you mean.
5. Click **Submit new issue**.

That's it. The maintainer is notified automatically and will respond.

### Path 2 — Propose exact wording (a "Pull Request")

Use this if you want to suggest the precise text of a change. It sounds technical, but the steps are straightforward, and **nothing you do here affects the live documents until the maintainer accepts it.**

1. **Fork the repository.** On the repository page, click **Fork** (top right). This creates your *own* personal copy. You are only ever editing your copy — never the original.
2. **Edit the file in your copy.** Open the document you want to change and click the pencil (✏️) icon to edit it directly in your browser.
3. **Describe and save your change.** Scroll down, write a short note explaining what you changed and why, and choose "Propose changes."
4. **Open the Pull Request.** GitHub will offer to open a **Pull Request** back to the original repository — this is your formal suggestion. Add a short explanation and submit it.

> [!NOTE]
> A "fork" is your own copy. A "Pull Request" is a polite request that says *"please consider pulling my change into your version."* It is a proposal only. The maintainer decides whether to accept it.

---

## What Happens to Your Suggestion

Every Issue and Pull Request is reviewed personally by the maintainer. You will receive one of three outcomes, normally with a brief note explaining the reasoning:

| Outcome | What it means |
|---|---|
| ✅ **Accepted as proposed** | Your suggestion is incorporated as you wrote it. |
| 🔧 **Accepted in principle, then adjusted** | The idea is taken up, but the maintainer may reword, reshape, or relocate it so it fits the framework's voice and structure. The final wording in the framework may differ from your draft. |
| ❌ **Declined** | The suggestion is not taken up this time. You will get a short reason. A decline is not a judgement on you — it usually means the change is out of scope, conflicts with a core principle, or doesn't suit the framework's purpose. |

Because the maintainer often reworks a good idea into the framework's own wording, your **exact text may not appear verbatim** even when your suggestion is welcomed and used. The credit and the thanks still stand.

---

## A Few Things That Help

- **Stay close to the purpose.** This framework is for keeping AI-assisted work organised, traceable, and under human control — and it is written for non-technical readers. Suggestions that protect that plain-language, practical spirit are the most likely to be accepted.
- **One idea per suggestion.** Smaller, focused suggestions are easier to review and act on than large rewrites.
- **Explain the "why."** A sentence on the problem your change solves is more persuasive than the change alone.
- **Be patient and kind.** This is reviewed by a person, around other work.
